# 850 words — learning tiers (priority, not alphabetic)

The 850 split into 5 Ogden classes. But for **learning order**, study them by leverage:

## Tier 1 — Operations (100) · learn FIRST

Highest leverage: verbs, pronouns, prepositions, connectives. Without these you can't build a sentence.
? already broken out in `../01-foundations/operators-18.md` + `directions-prepositions.md`.

## Tier 2 — Picturable things (200) · learn EARLY

Concrete, image-able nouns (`dog, hand, door, tree, cup`). Easiest to memorize because each maps to a picture.
Ogden built *English Through Pictures* on exactly these. ? drill with `../04-practice/english-through-pictures-book1.pdf`.

## Tier 3 — General things (400) · learn STEADILY

Abstract nouns (`reason, amount, condition, system`). Biggest chunk; spread over time.
Many pair with operators to replace verbs: `give attention`, `make a comparison`, `have a look`.

## Tier 4 — General qualities (100) · learn WITH things

Adjectives (`good, long, clear, possible`). Attach `-LY` for adverbs, `MORE/MOST` for degree.

## Tier 5 — Opposites (50) · learn AS PAIRS

Memorize against their Tier-4 partners (`good/bad`, `long/short`, `awake/asleep`).
