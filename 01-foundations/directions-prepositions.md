# Directions & prepositions (the second half of the skeleton)

These come from the **Operations (100)** class. Combined with the 18 operators they generate
phrasal verbs; on their own they handle place, time, and relation.

## Prepositions / directions (used with operators)

```
about, across, after, against, among, at, before, between, by,
down, from, in, off, on, over, through, to, under, up, with
```

## Relational / function words

```
as, for, of, till, than
```

## Directional & time adverbs

```
again, ever, far, forward, here, near, now, out, still, then,
there, together, well, back
north, south, east, west
tomorrow, yesterday
```

## Determiners, quantifiers, pronouns, connectives (rest of Operations)

```
a, the, all, any, every, little, much, no, other, some, such, that, this
I, he, you, who
and, because, but, or, if, though, while
how, when, where, why
almost, enough, even, not, only, quite, so, very
please, yes
```

## Highest-leverage 20 to drill first

`up, down, in, out, on, off, over, through, to, from, by, with, together, away*, back, here, there, now, then, not`

> *away is built (`a-` + `way`) / treated as a direction in usage; Ogden lists `way` + `a`.

## How directions multiply operators

| Direction | + get | + put | + take | + go |
|-----------|-------|-------|--------|------|
| up | get up (rise) | put up | take up | go up |
| off | get off | put off (delay) | take off (remove) | go off |
| out | get out | put out (extinguish) | take out | go out |
| in | get in | put in | take in (absorb) | go in (enter) |
| back | get back (return) | put back | take back | go back |
| on | get on (proceed) | put on (wear) | — | go on (continue) |
